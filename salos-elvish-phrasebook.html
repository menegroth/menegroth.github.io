What can be said about this little book? Three things jump out:

  • The language presented can only be called Sindarin if we use the loosest definition of that term. Much of what Salo writes is pure conjecture. He uses words that appear nowhere in Tolkien’s works and are in fact contradicted by them, e.g. hanna- for thank.
  • The phrases have a consistent aesthetic.
  • Salo has an uncanny knack for creating beautifully tuned phrases. We see two phrases that are identical in grammatical structure but have different word order: cuio vae vs. mae losto. Why switch the adverb and verb around in one case? There’s no grammatical reason to do so, Salo is just playing with the language, and in his hands its beauty shines like a jewel.
