Menegroth ✶ The Thousand Caves

A place for enthusiasts of J.R.R. Tolkien's invented languages

Mae govannen and mára omentië! Among these pages you'll find many articles relating to the Elven tongues of Arda, the invented world of J.R.R. Tolkien.

Essays on The Languages of J.R.R. Tolkien

  • Elvish Is Dead; Long Live Elvish! – A memoir of my experiences with Tolkien's languages, reflecting on why you can't (and can) and should (and shouldn't) learn Elvish.
  • Concerning Stress Placement in Sindarin – I explore the implications for Sindarin pronun­ciation of two poems from The Lord of the Rings.

Resources for Language Enthusiasts

  • A Ranger's Guide to Sindarin – A description of Sindarin grammar and vocabulary, focusing on the examples (mainly in LotR) that J.R.R.T. chose to publish.

External Links of Interest

  • Elvish as She Is Spoke, by Carl F. Hostetter – An overview of the difficulties facing the aspiring student of Elvish, and a cogent suggestion for how to overcome them. Hosted by the Elvish Linguistic Fellowship.
  • Ardalambion (Of the Tongues of Arda) – Helge Fauskanger's site covering many aspects of Tolkien's linguistic creations. His Quenya language course can be found there.
  • Parma Tyelpelassiva (Book of Silver Leaves) – Thorsten Renk's site containing Sindarin and Quenya courses, plus many fan-made compositions in Elvish.
